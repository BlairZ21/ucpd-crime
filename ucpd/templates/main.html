Crime on Campus

By Sahil Chinoy and Thomas Kim

November 18, 2015

In 2014, UC Berkeley had the second-greatest number of violent crimes of any University of California campus, behind only Los Angeles, according to the FBI. It also experienced more than 1,000 property crimes such as burglary and theft — the most of any of the 49 California public schools in the FBI data.

On a campus with more than 35,000 students located in an urban area, it may be unsurprising that UC Berkeley has a relatively high crime rate compared to smaller schools in suburban settings. But crime reports are not distributed evenly throughout the campus or during the day, a Daily Californian analysis of five years of UCPD's daily logs showed. While violent crime clusters on the outskirts of campus, most property crime reports originate on campus and are concentrated during the weekday daytime hours.

By area

The area south of campus has experienced the most violent crime reports since 2010, notably in People's Park and the surrounding Telegraph Avenue area, where there were multiple reports of battery, aggravated theft, robbery and assault. However, the crimes reported most often in the area were quality-of-life crimes — namely drug and alcohol violations and disorderly conduct — and petty theft.

In contrast, most property crimes were reported on campus. Frequent reports came from the Recreational Sports Facility, Golden Bear Cafe, Dwinelle Hall and libraries such as Doe Library and Moffitt Library, areas where students and staff are more likely to leave personal items unattended.

By time of day

Although crime reports tended to be more frequent on weekday afternoons, they were largely property crimes. Violent crime reports were more sporadic.

By month

Crime reports have decreased since 2010, though they exhibit strong seasonal variation. Property and quality-of-life crime reports declined during the summer months, likely because there were fewer people on and around campus.
