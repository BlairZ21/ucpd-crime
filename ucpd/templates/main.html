Crime on Campus

By Sahil Chinoy and Thomas Kim | Staff

November 13, 2015

The Daily Californian analyzed five years of UCPD crime logs. Explore an interactive map of crime around campus, view crime by time of day, see historical trends or read more about how we analyzed the data.

By area

Our analysis showed that the area with consistent violent crime numbers is in the Southside area of Berkeley - most notably in People's Park. In the context of violent crime, we found that historically there were several cases of battery, aggravated theft, robbery, and assault in People's Park. However, we also observed that these cases were few and far between and the most common crime occurring in People's Park was either drug or alcohol related or minor incidents like petty theft.

Interestingly, the most common area for property theft was at the RSF, Golden Bear Cafe, Dwinelle Hall and in libraries such as Doe and Moffitt. We suspect that this is because students and university staff members are more likely to leave personal items in these areas unattended.

By time of day

Although crime reports tended to be more frequent on weekday afternoons, they were largely incidents of property crime — theft and burglary, as opposed to violent crime such as assault, which was more sporadic.

By month

Crime reports have decreased since 2010, though they exhibit a strong seasonal variation. Property and quality-of-life crime tends to drop during the summer months, likely because there are fewer people on and around campus.
