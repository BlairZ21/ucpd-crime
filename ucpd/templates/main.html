Crime on Campus

By Sahil Chinoy and Thomas Kim | Staff

November 17, 2015

The Daily Californian analyzed five years of UCPD crime logs. Explore an interactive map of crime around campus, view crime by time of day, see historical trends or read more about how we analyzed the data.

By area

The area south of campus has experienced the most violent crime reports since 2010, notably in People's Park and the surrounding area, where there were multiple reports of battery, aggravated theft, robbery and assault. However, the most crimes reported most often in the area were drug and alcohol violations and petty theft.

Most property crimes were reported on campus — at the Recreational Sports Facility, Golden Bear Cafe, Dwinelle Hall and in libraries such as Doe Library and Moffitt Library, areas where students and staff are more likely to leave personal items unattended.

By time of day

Although crime reports tended to be more frequent on weekday afternoons, they were largely property crimes such as theft and burglary. Violent crime reports were more sporadic.

By month

Crime reports have decreased since 2010, though they exhibit a strong seasonal variation. Property and quality-of-life crime reports declined during the summer months, likely because there were fewer people on and around campus.
