We filed a California Public Records Act request with UCPD and reviewed daily incident logs from January 2010 to September 2015.

To analyze crime by incident type, we created a mapping of incident descriptions to one of three categories: violent crime, such as assault and robbery, property crime, such as theft and vandalism and quality-of-life crime, such as alcohol and drug violations. Though we attempted to follow the FBI's Uniform Crime Reporting standards, the classification involved judging how to categorize some crime types.

Many of the logged events were security checks, alarms or escorts. We excluded those incidents from our analysis. In all, we analyzed 9,204 of the 28,138 reports.

To put the incidents on a map, we had to assign each point a latitude and longitude, a process known as geocoding. We were able to geocode 8,175 incidents, 7,643 of which were close to campus.

The data and code used for this project is available to the public.
